01 / About
The short version

A product leader who speaks engineering.

Most product managers translate between business and engineering. I grew up on the other side of that wall — as a developer shipping games, simulators, and platforms — before stepping into product. That background means I don't just write requirements; I understand the cost, the trade-offs, and the feasibility of every decision before it reaches the backlog.

"I go beyond executing tasks — I analyze and prioritize the problem, drive discovery, define the solution, and own the outcome."

Over 11+ years I've taken products from a blank page through discovery, MVP, launch, and iteration — a Web3 gaming ecosystem with a 100,000+ player mobile title, an AR tourist-guide platform in Dubai, a high-fidelity VR training simulator, and enterprise XR software on HoloLens. The thread through all of it: data-, research-, and user-driven decisions, followed through to implementation.

Operating principle 01
Outcomes over output. A shipped feature that doesn't move a metric is a cost, not a win.
Operating principle 02
Feasibility is a feature. Knowing the stack lets me kill bad ideas early and protect good ones.
Operating principle 03
Discovery before delivery. The hardest part of product is choosing the right problem to solve.
Operating principle 04
Quality is followed through. I stay close to implementation so the vision survives contact with code.
